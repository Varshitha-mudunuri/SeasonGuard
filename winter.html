Winter Season Diseases

Information on common winter diseases, their symptoms, cures, medications, and prevention tips.

Common Cold

Symptoms

Runny nose, sore throat, cough, congestion, slight body aches, mild headache.

Medication & Cure

OTC medications like antihistamines and decongestants; rest, hydration, and warm liquids.

Prevention

Wash hands frequently, avoid close contact with sick people, strengthen immunity.

Influenza (Flu)

Symptoms

Fever, chills, muscle aches, cough, congestion, runny nose, fatigue.

Medication & Cure

Antiviral medications if prescribed; rest, fluids, and pain relievers for comfort.

Prevention

Annual flu vaccination, hygiene practices, staying warm, avoiding crowded places.

Pneumonia

Symptoms

Chest pain, cough, fever, chills, difficulty breathing, fatigue.

Medication & Cure

Antibiotics for bacterial pneumonia, antivirals for viral causes; supportive care.

Prevention

Vaccination, good hygiene, avoiding smoke, and maintaining overall health.

Bronchitis

Symptoms

Persistent cough, mucus production, fatigue, shortness of breath, slight fever, and chills.

Medication & Cure

Cough suppressants, bronchodilators, rest, and fluids; antibiotics if bacterial.

Prevention

Avoid smoke, wash hands regularly, use a humidifier, and protect against colds and flu.

Strep Throat

Symptoms

Sore throat, difficulty swallowing, swollen tonsils, fever, and red spots in the mouth.

Medication & Cure

Antibiotics if bacterial, warm liquids, and pain relievers.

Prevention

Avoid close contact with infected people, wash hands frequently, and avoid sharing utensils.

Norovirus

Symptoms

Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach pain, and fever.

Medication & Cure

Hydration with fluids and electrolytes; no specific medication as it typically resolves on its own.

Prevention

Wash hands often, disinfect surfaces, avoid contaminated food and water.

Asthma Exacerbation

Symptoms

Shortness of breath, wheezing, coughing, and chest tightness.

Medication & Cure

Inhalers, corticosteroids, and bronchodilators as prescribed by a doctor.

Prevention

Avoid cold air exposure, wear a scarf over the nose and mouth, and avoid allergens and smoke.
