Summer Season Diseases

A comprehensive guide on common summer season diseases, their symptoms, cures, medications, and preventive measures.

Common Summer Season Diseases

Chickenpox

Symptoms

Itchy red spots, fever, fatigue, loss of appetite, headache.

Medication & Cure

Rest, calamine lotion, cool baths, antiviral medications if severe.

Prevention

Vaccination, avoid close contact with infected individuals.

Sunburn

Symptoms

Red, painful skin, swelling, blistering, peeling.

Medication & Cure

Aloe vera gel, cool compresses, moisturizers, and pain relievers.

Prevention

Apply sunscreen, avoid peak sun hours, wear protective clothing.

Typhoid Fever

Symptoms

High fever, stomach pain, fatigue, diarrhea or constipation, headache.

Medication & Cure

Antibiotics, hydration, and medical care.

Prevention

Drink safe water, practice good hygiene, avoid raw food in contaminated areas.

Prickly Heat

Symptoms

Red, itchy skin with small blisters, usually in areas prone to sweating.

Medication & Cure

Cool showers, loose clothing, anti-itch creams.

Prevention

Stay cool, wear breathable fabrics, avoid excessive sweating.

Food Poisoning

Symptoms

Stomach cramps, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and fever.

Medication & Cure

Rest, hydration, and sometimes antibiotics if necessary.

Prevention

Keep food refrigerated, avoid undercooked foods, and maintain hand hygiene.

Heat Rash

Symptoms

Red bumps on skin, itching, or prickling sensation.

Medication & Cure

Keep skin cool, apply talcum powder, and wear loose clothing.

Prevention

Stay in a cool, dry place, avoid excessive sweating, and wear light clothing.

Acute Gastroenteritis

Symptoms

Severe stomach pain, diarrhea, vomiting, dehydration.

Medication & Cure

Hydration, oral rehydration solution (ORS), and in severe cases, antibiotics.

Prevention

Ensure clean drinking water, maintain food hygiene, and avoid street food.

Conjunctivitis (Pink Eye)

Symptoms

Redness in the eye, itching, discharge, watery eyes.

Medication & Cure

Eye drops, cold compresses, and in severe cases, antibiotic drops.

Prevention

Avoid touching the eyes, maintain hand hygiene, and avoid sharing personal items.

Heat Exhaustion

Symptoms

Heavy sweating, weakness, dizziness, nausea, muscle cramps.

Medication & Cure

Rest in a cool place, hydration, cool compresses, and electrolyte-rich drinks.

Prevention

Stay hydrated, avoid overexertion, and take breaks in shaded areas during high temperatures.

Allergic Reactions

Symptoms

Sneezing, runny nose, itchy eyes, skin rash, or swelling.

Medication & Cure

Antihistamines, nasal sprays, and avoid exposure to allergens.

Prevention

Identify and avoid allergens, keep indoor air clean, and practice good hygiene.
